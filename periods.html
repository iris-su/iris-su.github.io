Periods


  1. What they are:
    1. Periods are a natural, normal body process for all females to become women and mature physically.

  2. How it works:
    1. Every month, for 3-7 days, our daughter will have her period. This means she’ll have to wear pads or tampons, to contain their menstrual stream.

  3. Pads vs tampons:
    1. Pads are disposable sanitary napkins that absorb the fluids that come out.
    2. Tampons are also like pads; however, instead of laying it out on the underwear, it goes inside the girl’s body.
      1. A suggestion is to just start with pads, and if they want to use tampons, they can later on.
    3. Your daughter needs to be able to angle it in towards the small of her back, otherwise she might harm herself.
    4. Your daughter has to change their pad or tampon every 6-8 hours, or else she may get a infection.

  4. How long it lasts/How to help:
    1. During this period of time, your daughter will be in distressed. She may get cramps, which will hurt A LOT.
    2. She will be emotional, so if she’s craving anything, make sure she gets what she wants.
      1. It might be a good idea to store your house with things she wants to eat.
      2. So if she’s on her period and in a bad mood, understand that she’s just on her period.

  5. How to talk to them:
    1. Although this subject may be very awkward, in the end, you are her father, so it shouldn’t be that bad. Periods are a very normal thing. You only need to talk to her about it a couple of times. Understand how she will act during her periods, and try to help her.
