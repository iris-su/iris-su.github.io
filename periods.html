Periods

  • What they are:
    • Periods are a natural, normal body process for all females to become women and mature physically.
  • How it works:
    • Every month, for 3-7 days, our daughter will have her period. This means she’ll have to wear pads or tampons, to contain their menstrual stream.
  • Pads vs tampons:
    • Pads are disposable sanitary napkins that absorb the fluids that come out.
    • Tampons are also like pads; however, instead of laying it out on the underwear, it goes inside the girl’s body.
      • Your daughter needs to be able to angle it in towards the small of her back, otherwise she might harm herself.
      • A suggestion is to just start with pads, and if they want to use tampons, they can later on.
    • Your daughter has to change their pad or tampon every 6-8 hours, or else she may get a infection.
  • How long it lasts/How to help:
    • During this period of time, your daughter will be in distressed. She may get cramps, which will hurt A LOT.
    • She will be emotional, so if she’s craving anything, make sure she gets what she wants.
      • It might be a good idea to store your house with things she wants to eat.
      • So if she’s on her period and in a bad mood, understand that she’s just on her period.
  • How to talk to them:
    • Although this subject may be very awkward, in the end, you are her father, so it shouldn’t be that bad. Periods are a very normal thing. You only need to talk to her about it a couple of times. Understand how she will act during her periods, and try to help her.
